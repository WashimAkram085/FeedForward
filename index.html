How we help?

Keep records of food waste in the hostel's kitchen and dining areas. Analyze the data to identify patterns and areas where waste occurs most frequently. This information can help you target specific issues.

Encourage feedback from residents and guests. Ask for their input on food quality, portion sizes, and any suggestions for reducing waste.

Why we help?

Food production has significant environmental consequences, including greenhouse gas emissions, deforestation, and habitat destruction. When food is wasted, all the resources used in its production, such as water, energy, and land, go to waste as well. Reducing food waste helps mitigate these environmental impacts and contributes to a more sustainable planet.

Food waste represents a significant economic cost. Households, businesses, and institutions spend money on food that ultimately ends up in the trash. By reducing waste, we can save money and allocate resources more efficiently.
